Child Protection & Safeguarding Policy

An unwavering commitment to creating a safe, secure, and nurturing online environment for every child.

Version: 2.1 Last Updated: 1 August 2025 Next Review: 1 August 2026

1. Overarching Commitment

Baobab Online Academy (hereafter "the Academy") fully accepts its responsibility to safeguard and promote the welfare of all children entrusted to its care. We believe that every child, regardless of age, disability, gender, racial heritage, religious belief, sexual orientation, or identity, has a right to equal protection from all types of harm or abuse. Our commitment is woven into every aspect of our operations, from tutor recruitment to our online platform's architecture.

2. Purpose of this Policy

  • To protect children who engage with the Academy's services online.
  • To provide parents, tutors, and staff with the overarching principles that guide our approach to child safeguarding.
  • To ensure all stakeholders understand their roles and responsibilities in safeguarding children.
  • To provide clear procedures for identifying and reporting concerns.

3. Personnel & Recruitment (Our 'Safe Recruitment' Promise)

3.1. Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

The Academy has a named Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) who is a senior member of staff. The DSL is the first point of contact for all safeguarding concerns. All staff and tutors are aware of the DSL's identity and how to contact them.

3.2. Tutor Vetting & Verification

We operate a rigorous, multi-stage vetting process for all Nigeria-based tutors, which includes, but is not limited to:

  1. Identity Verification: Thorough checks of government-issued identification.
  2. Criminal Background Checks: Mandatory, enhanced checks appropriate to their jurisdiction.
  3. Reference Checks: Professional and character references are meticulously followed up.
  4. Qualification Verification: All academic and teaching qualifications are verified at the source.
  5. Safeguarding Interview: A specific, scenario-based interview to assess their understanding and commitment to child protection.

3.3. Continuous Training

All tutors and staff undergo mandatory safeguarding training upon induction and receive annual refresher courses. This training covers online safety, signs of abuse, reporting procedures, and the Academy's code of conduct.

4. Code of Conduct & Safe Practices

All tutors must adhere to a strict code of conduct designed for the online environment.

  • All tutoring sessions must take place via the official, monitored Baobab Academy platform. Communication outside this platform is strictly forbidden.
  • All one-on-one sessions are recorded for safeguarding and quality assurance purposes, accessible to the DSL and parents via the portal.
  • Professional language and conduct are required at all times.
  • Tutors must not request or share personal contact information (email, phone, social media) with students or parents.
  • Tutors must maintain a professional appearance and ensure their teaching environment is appropriate and free from distractions.

5. Reporting Concerns

We foster a culture of openness where any concern, no matter how small, is taken seriously.

5.1. Procedure for Parents & Students

If a parent or student has any concern about a tutor's conduct or any aspect of their online experience, they must report it immediately. Concerns can be raised through:

  1. The 'Report Concern' button: A discreet button is available within the session interface and on the main portal dashboard. This sends an immediate, confidential alert to the DSL.
  2. Direct Contact with the DSL: The DSL's contact details are made clearly available within this policy and in the 'Support' section of the portal.

5.2. Our Response

Upon receiving a concern, the DSL will immediately initiate a formal investigation. Actions may include session review, interviews with relevant parties, and temporary suspension of a tutor pending the outcome. We are committed to working with parents and, where necessary, statutory child protection agencies to ensure the child's welfare.
