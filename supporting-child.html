Supporting Your Child's Learning

Your partnership is the soil in which your child's education grows. Discover how you can create a nurturing environment for academic success and cultural pride.

Create a Conducive Learning Space

A dedicated, quiet space can significantly improve focus and retention. It signals that it's time to learn.

  • Choose a well-lit, quiet area.
  • Ensure it's free from distractions like TV or toys.
  • Organize supplies (notebooks, pens, headset) for easy access.
  • Establish this space as a "learning zone" used consistently.

Engage Actively with the Portal

The portal is your window into their world. Regular check-ins help you stay informed and proactive.

  • Log in daily to view announcements.
  • Review progress reports with your child weekly.
  • Celebrate achievements and discuss areas for improvement.
  • Use feedback from tutors to guide conversations.

Practice Positive Communication

Your words have power. Focus on effort and growth, not just grades, to build resilience and a love for learning.

  • Ask open-ended questions about their day.
  • Praise their hard work and perseverance.
  • Frame challenges as opportunities to learn.
  • Listen to their concerns and celebrate their curiosity.

Extend Cultural Learning

Connect what they learn online to your family's daily life. This makes heritage tangible and meaningful.

  • Cook traditional meals together.
  • Share family stories and histories.
  • Listen to music or watch films from your heritage.
  • Practice speaking your native language at home.

Build a Partnership with Tutors

Think of tutors as your partners. Proactive, respectful communication builds a strong support system.

  • Prepare for scheduled tutor calls.
  • Share insights about your child's learning style.
  • Ask for specific ways you can help at home.
  • Provide constructive feedback respectfully.

Promote Digital Wellbeing

Balance is crucial. Help your child develop healthy habits for their time both on and off the screen.

  • Establish clear start and end times for online learning.
  • Encourage physical activity and outdoor play.
  • Ensure they take regular breaks to rest their eyes.
  • Engage in offline family activities.
